## What to Keep vs. What to Replace

**PRESERVE — Logo:**
Harper's has an established logo: a circular vintage badge with a sun/ray motif, "HARPER'S" in bold display lettering, and "Restaurant & Brewpub" as the subtitle. The logo has warm amber/orange tones. **Do not redesign the logo.** Use it prominently — it is the anchor of the brand identity and should appear in the navbar and hero.

**PRESERVE — Navigation structure:**
Home · About Us · Menus · Apply Now · Gallery · Location & Contact

**PRESERVE — Copy and messaging (use this verbatim or close to it):**
- "Harper's Restaurant & Brewpub"
- "Family Owned"
- "Local Farm-to-Table"
- "Tank-to-Table Freshness"
- "Dishes Made from Scratch"
- "Homemade Burgers"
- "Farm-to-Table Ingredients"
- "We serve premium beer, food & baked goods made by a team of professionals"
- "Hand-Crafted Gluten Reduced Beer"
- "Harper's Gives Back"
- "Our Sustainability Initiatives"
- "We Do Parties + Special Events"
- "Meet Our Most Popular Pizza — Spinach Pesto"
- Happy Hour messaging (daily happy hour)
- "Check us out on Instagram"

**REPLACE ENTIRELY — Visual design:**
The current site uses teal/turquoise section headers, coral-red clip-art badge graphics, and a white background layout that looks like a 2012 restaurant template. **None of this should carry forward.** The new design should feel like a completely different, premium venue. A visitor who saw the old site and the new site side by side should not believe they're the same bar.

## About Harper's

Harper's is the defining bar of East Lansing's college scene. Founded in 1997, it sits at the heart of Michigan State University's social life. It reaches capacity nearly every weekend and serves as the undisputed go-to Game Day bar for MSU football, the top Friday and Saturday night destination, and the preferred venue for Greek life and social events. It is not a dive bar — it is a premium, high-energy destination with two distinct personalities: a stunning outdoor patio with string lights, a wood pergola draped in vines, and upscale casual dining; and a packed, dark, fog-machine nightclub interior with a DJ booth, concert-level lighting, and wall-to-wall crowds. It also has an on-site brewery with copper fermentation tanks visible from the interior.

---

## Design Direction

The website should feel **premium and lively** — the way a high-end magazine would cover a college bar. Think editorial photography layouts, bold typography, rich dark tones cut with warm amber and vibrant color pops. It should feel like a place that's genuinely worth showing up to on a Friday night. The vibe is: Soho House energy meets Big Ten college town.

---

## Brand Colors — New Palette

Extracted from Harper's actual brand assets (hats, hoodies, branded cups) and photography:

- **Primary:** Deep forest green (~#1E3A2F) — pulled from the Harper's East Lansing hat and hoodie embroidery. This is the brand's true color, not the current teal.
- **Secondary:** Warm cream/off-white (~#F5EFE0) — from the hat and hoodie colorway. Pairs elegantly with the green.
- **Accent:** Warm amber/copper (~#B5733A) — from the copper brew tanks and interior wood tones. Use for highlight text, dividers, subtle UI details.
- **Nightlife accent:** Electric blue-purple — from the DJ fog machine and stage lighting in the nightlife photos. Use sparingly for the nightlife section only.
- **Dark background:** Near-black (~#0D0D0D) or very deep charcoal — for nightlife sections and the overall site canvas where dark is appropriate.
- **Light background:** Warm off-white (#F5EFE0) or light warm grey — for food, patio, and about sections.
- **Text:** Off-white on dark; deep charcoal (#1A1A1A) on light.

**What to retire from the current site:** The teal/turquoise is gone. The coral-red badge graphics are gone. The flat white #FFFFFF background is gone.

---

## Typography

- **Primary display / headings:** Complement the existing logo with an elegant, editorial serif or high-character script — something that feels like it belongs on a vintage varsity jacket or a premium bar menu. A refined display serif (Playfair Display, Freight Display, or similar) or a script that echoes the logo's style without competing with it.
- **Subheadings:** Clean, confident, modern semi-bold sans-serif (Inter, Neue Haas Grotesk, or similar). Creates premium contrast against the display headings.
- **Body copy:** Lightweight, highly readable sans-serif. Generous line height. Clean and modern, not decorative.
- **The contrast between script/serif headings and clean sans body is intentional.** It signals heritage and quality without feeling stuffy.

## Page Sections

1. **Nav** — Minimal, sticky. Harper's logo centered or left. Links right. Dark background (near-black or deep green). Clean and elegant. No clutter.

2. **Hero** — Full-screen. Full-bleed background photo (the bartender pour shot, the patio dinner scene, or the Harper's sign + fog shot are strong candidates). Harper's logo overlaid large. Short punchy tagline: "East Lansing's Favorite Bar Since 1997" or "Est. 1997 · East Lansing, MI." One CTA button (View Menu or See What's On).

3. **About / The Vibe** — Short copy section. Two or three sentences about what Harper's is. Support with 2–3 photo tiles in an asymmetric grid — mix patio feel with nightlife energy. The dual personality is the story.

4. **The Patio** — Dedicated full-width section. Lead with the patio dinner scene or the hats-on-table photo. String lights, pergola, flowers, warm evening light. Copy: "Our patio is East Lansing's favorite outdoor dining and social space." Should feel like a destination on its own.

5. **Food & Drinks** — Editorial photo grid. Feature the pizzas (spinach pesto prominently — it's the most popular), the bartender pour, the drink cheers shot, the food table overhead. Include "Tank-to-Table Freshness," "Made from Scratch," and "Homemade Burgers" messaging. Should make someone hungry immediately.

6. **Game Day** — Dark, bold, high-energy section. Game Day crowd photos. Big display typography: "The #1 Game Day Bar in East Lansing." MSU football context. Packed. Electric.

7. **Nightlife** — The most immersive section. Dark background, full-bleed photos. DJ overhead, Harper's sign + fog, packed dance floor fisheye. Should feel like you're inside it. Consider a dark reveal or fade-in as the user scrolls in.

8. **Events** — Clean, minimal. "We Do Parties + Special Events." Greek life, private bookings, performer nights. Simple CTA to inquire.

9. **Brewery** — Hoodies + copper brew tanks photo. "Hand-Crafted, Tank-to-Table." Brief copy about the on-site brewery. This differentiates Harper's from a standard bar.

10. **Merch** — Harper's hats and hoodies. Clean product-style layout. "Show Your Harper's Love." Link to merch.

11. **Harper's Gives Back** — Brief section. "Family Owned. Community Minded." Sustainability messaging.

12. **Find Us** — Location, hours, contact. Google Maps embed. Clean and minimal.

13. **Footer** — Instagram CTA. Logo. Nav links. Copyright.

## Photography Notes

All provided photos are **vertical (portrait orientation)**. Full creative freedom:
- Crop any photo to horizontal/landscape for hero or full-width sections — subjects are well-centered and cropping works
- Stack photos 2 or 3 side-by-side in editorial grid layouts
- Use as full-bleed backgrounds with dark overlay and text on top
- Vary scale dramatically within sections — one giant image next to two smaller stacked ones
- Mask or clip into creative shapes if it serves the layout
- **The photos are the hero of this design. Build the layout around them, not the other way around.**

---

## Animations & Interactions

- Parallax scrolling on hero and major section transitions
- Staggered photo fade/slide-in on scroll for grid sections
- Subtle hover: scale-up or brightness reveal on gallery images
- Section transitions feel cinematic — dark wipe or fade into the nightlife section
- Sticky nav transitions from transparent to solid on scroll
- Optional: fine film grain or noise texture overlay on dark sections for depth and texture

## Tone

Confident. Premium. Fun. Unapologetically East Lansing. The kind of bar you tell people about. Not trying to be fancy — it just is.

## What to Avoid

- The current teal/turquoise color scheme — it's gone
- Clip-art badge graphics (the scratch/burgers/ingredients stamps from the current site)
- Generic restaurant website templates
- Anything that looks like it was built before 2018
- Flat white backgrounds throughout — use warm tones and dark sections with intention
- Cluttered layouts — let images breathe with generous whitespace
- Cheesy "Book Your Event" banners — make inquiries feel organic and elevated
